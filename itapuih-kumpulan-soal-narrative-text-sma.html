Narrative Text

One day in the forest which was still green and beautiful, the horse at that time still had horns. He proudly reflected in the clear lake in the middle of the forest while admiring his appearance with horns like a king's crown. At that time a deer appeared, which at that time had no horns, joined the mirror and drank in the lake. However, he was immediately chased away by the horse who felt that the deer only interfered with the view and interfered with the enjoyment. The deer sadly left.

Suddenly, from a distance, there was the sound of a tiger roaring loudly, as if to shake the entire dwellers of the quiet forest. The birds were startled flying, all the forest dwellers were hiding in fear. The horse and deer fled as fast as they could. The deer are agile and able to run among the bushes and tree branches. But unfortunately the horse horns caught in a tree branch and screamed for help. Nobody wants to help.

Suddenly, from the bush, unexpectedly a deer appeared, quietly nibbling on a tree branch that was wrapped around a horse's horn. The horse was touched and did not expect the deer's kindness.

When they finally got out of the danger, the horse found it helpful and presented its horns to the deer in gratitude. That's the origin of the deer having antlers.

Pembahasan

Narrative text adalah teks yang menceritakan urutan kejadian yang dialami pelaku dalam teks tersebut, baik itu fiksi maupun non fiksi.

Dalam soal di atas yang dijadikan narrative text adalah cerita fiksi berbentuk fabel yang diperankan oleh binatang penghuni hutan. Dalam narrative text ada empat unsur, yaitu:

Orientation yang merupakan pengenalan awal cerita. Complication yang merupakan awal datangnya masalah. Resolution yaitu pemecahan masalah yang dialami oleh tokoh cerita. Re-orientation yaitu kesimpulan atas seluruh cerita atau berisi pesan cerita tersebut. Pelajari lebih lanjut

Jawaban:

1. Narrative text is a text that tells about sequence of events in the past.

2. The purpose of narrative text is to entertain the reader with the story which can be fiction or non-fiction.

3. There are three generic structure of narrative text, namely: Orientation, Complication, and Resolution.

4. Language of narrative text:

– Past tense (killed, drunk, etc)

– Adverb of time (Once upun a time, one day, etc)

– Time conjunction (when, then, suddenly, etc)

– Specific character. The character of the story is specific, not general. (Cinderella, Snow White, Alibaba, etc)

– Action verbs. A verb that shows an action. (killed, dug, walked, etc)

– Direct speech. It is to make the story lively. (Snow White said,”My name is Snow White). The direct speech uses present tense.

7. kumpulan text narrative beserta soal dan jawaban


Narrative Text

In the forest the fastest running animal is the rabbit. No one can beat him. The rabbit arrogantly posed a challenge to all forest dwellers to be able to defeat him.

When none of the other animals dared to accept his challenge, suddenly the turtle, which was known to walk very slowly, said he had accepted the challenge. One forest in unison laughed at him in disbelief. Moreover, the rabbit who was so arrogant underestimated the ability of the turtle.

On the day of the start of the match the rabbit began to show his ability to run very fast at the beginning of the match. The turtle began to walk quietly and slowly, as if ignoring the ability and arrogance of the rabbit.

The rabbit because he felt that he had the upper hand after running a long distance, he walked leisurely, then sat under a tree. The very cool wind apparently lulled the rabbit to sleep.

Apparently the turtle although walking very slowly but he never stopped. Until finally he passed the rabbit who was asleep under a tree.

Finally the rabbit woke up and chased the turtle, but unfortunately his efforts were in vain, because the turtle had already reached the finish line and became the winner. The rabbit realized his arrogance.

The fable above gives a message to us that high ability if not accompanied by humility will only produce arrogance. On the other hand, ordinary abilities but accompanied by perseverance will definitely produce real results.

The story above is a part of narrative text. You can find narrative text in many forms, including stories: fictions and non fictions, fables, or biographies.

The best way to learn a narrative text is by reading many stories in English then answering questions about the text. Or if there is no question you can create your own question to get a better comprehension of the text.

In the the text above we have created the following answers:

The fastest animal in the forest was the rabbit. The rabbit's challenge was anyone who can beat him in speed. The all forest laugh at the turtle because he wanted to beat the fast rabbit with his slow speed. The rabbit fell asleep under the tree. The turtle defeated the fast rabbit by his patience and perseverance. I can learn from the story that the patience and perseverance can beat arrogances and skill. Pelajari lebih lanjut

Aladdin and the Magic LampOnce upon a time, there lived a handsome boy in Baghdad. The name was Aladdin. He was a clever boy, but he did not like to work.

One day Aladdin’s mother sent him to the market place to look for a job. In the market he met a magician. The magician asked him to work together with him. If Aladdin could do, he would get a lot of gold coins and jewellery. The magician asked Aladdin to go inside the cave and find a magical lamp there. He also gave a ring and said, “If you are in any trouble, rub this ring and a genie will come to your rescue!”

Aladdin went into the cave. Inside the cave, Aladdin was surprised by what he saw. Every corner was full of gold and treasures. A few minutes later, he found the lamp and quickly returned to the entrance of the cave. Aladdin called out, “Magician, I have found the lamp.” “Excellent,” answered the magician. “Quick, pass it to me!”

Aladdin did not trust the magician so he said, “Not so fast Magician. You must help me out of this cave first.”

The magician became very angry. When Aladdin was walking to the entrance, the magician pushed a huge rock over the entrance of the cave and left Aladdin and the lamp behind. Aladdin tried to move the rock all by himself, but it was too big and heavy, “Oh no! I am trapped in the cave!” he cried.

Suddenly, he remembered the magic ring. He rubbed it with the palm of his hand. To his surprise, a genie appeared before him and in a powerful voice said, “Master, I am the Genie of the ring. How can I help you?”

Aladdin was frightened, but he soon found the courage to ask, “Could you please send me back to my house?” In no time at all Aladdin was back home.

Aladdin took out the lamp. He wondered what would happen if he rubbed it. As Aladdin was rubbing it, all of a sudden another enormous genie stood before him. The genie of the lamp said, “Master, your wish is my command.” Aladdin was very excited and wished for an enormous palace and bags full of gold.

One day, a beautiful princess was passing by Aladdin’s palace. She stopped to talk to Aladdin and soon they became good friends. Before long, Aladdin and Jasmine fell in love each other. She married Aladdin and lived happily.

But no one came, and so the starving merchant sat down to a hearty meal.  
Overcome by curiosity, he ventured upstairs, where the corridor led into magnificent rooms and halls. A fire crackled in the first room and a soft bed looked very inviting. It was now late, and the merchant could not resist. He lay down on the bed and fell fast asleep.

But, as on the evening before, there was nobody in sight. Shaking his head in wonder at the strangeness of it all, he went towards the garden where he had left his horse, tethered to a tree.

One day, the king was traveling. He was so busy counting his gold . He didn’t notice that he was passing through the Sherwood Forest, the home of Robin Hood. Nor did he see two pairs of eyes that watching him from the tree.

“Look at those gold, Little John! Let’s pay the King a little visit, shall we?” Robin said.

Inside the coach, the King was singing happily.

“Golden Golden, I love things made of Gold… from top of my crown ‘till the tip of my toes”

Robin hood were standing by the road-side as he disguised himself as a fortune teller. The King saw him and got interested. Therefore he asked ‘the lady’ to tell him about his fortune.

“Ooh~ A long life with lots and lots of gold. Such a wonderful future” Robin said.

“Of course! Gold is the most beautiful thing in the world” The King said excitedly. He didn’t realize that Little John is taking his gold.

And so, the two manage to get all of the gold without getting caught.

Meanwhile, in the village, the Sheriff was busy collecting taxes.

“I must have more taxes! Hand over those gold!” the Sheriff said.

“No! please don’t! We need these gold!” a girl said.

“This is the king’s order! You don’t want to get arrested, do you?” the Sheriff said.

“Hu huhu… No… But… uh-!” in the end, The Sheriff took the gold by force.

Not long after The Sheriff left, Robin came.

“What’s wrong little girl? Why are you crying?” Robin asked.

“The Sheriff… He took all of our golds.” The girl said.

“Oo De Lally~! you don’t have to worry then, little girl. Here, I have a lot more for you” Robin said.

“this one for you, this one for you, this one for you, and this one, woops, I think I just ran out of gold. Well then, for you my lady, what about a rose?”

In the end, everyone’s happy.

Later then, Little John told Robin Hood that the King was having an archery tournament by tomorrow. And he promised a great prize for the winner. Hearing this, Robin Hood decided to join in.

The next day, the tournament was held. It was actually not an ordinary tournament. The King was planning on capturing Robin Hood because he knew that Robin hood was good at archery.

But, Robin Hood was smart. He came with another disguise so no one notice that he was Robin Hood. The tournament then soon began.

Most of the contestant had failed. And now, there’s only 2 people with one of them was Robin Hood.

The first contestant shot. but it didn’t hit the target. And now, it’s Robin’s turn. He takes a deep breath before then he shot. It hits the bullseye! The crowds went hype.

And that is when, the Sheriff realize. “Get that man! He is Robin Hood!” he shouted. But it was all too late, because Robin Hood had successfully escaped. Unknown to the Sheriff and the king, their gold had being taken once again

After all, the tournament was only a distraction...
