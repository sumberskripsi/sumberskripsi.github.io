The Legend of Kesodo Feast in Mount Bromo
Hundreds years ago, the last king of Majapahit had a young princess from one of his wives. The princess was named Roro Anteng. Later, Roro Anteng married Joko Seger, who came from Brahman caste.

Because of unfortunate situation the couple was forced to leave the kingdom. They settled in the mountain area. They ruled the area and named it Tengger, which was derived from their names, Roro Anteng and Joko Seger.

After several years of reign, the region flourished in prosperity, but Roro Anteng and Joko Seger were sad because they did not have a child. They climbed the top of mountain and prayed there night and day hoping that the God would listen. The prayer was heard and Betoro Bromo promised to give them many children. However, they had to promise that they would sacrifice their youngest child in return.

Roro Anteng  gave birth to a child, then another and another. In the end, Roro Anteng and Joko Seger had 25 children. Soon it was time to sacrifice the youngest child , Kusuma, but the parents could not do it. They tried to hide him, but an eruption happened and Kusuma fell into the crater. There was silence before they heard a voice. “I have to be sacrificed so that you will all stay alive. From now on, you should arrange an annual offering ceremony on the 14th of Kesodo (the twelfth month of Tengerese calendar)”. It was Kusuma’s voice. Kusuma’s brothers and sisters held the offering ceremony every year. Instead of human being, they collected fruits, vegetables, rice, and meat to be off to the Gods. And this has been done generation after generation until today.

20. tolong buatkan contoh soal narative text dari cerita sangkuriang aku belum faham


legend sangkuriang
A long time ago, the ancient land of Sunda was ruled by a king and a queen who had but a single daughter. Her name was Dayang Sumbi. She was beautiful and clever but also pampered and spoiled. One day as she was weaving in her pavilion, she became moody and distracted, which caused her to keep dropping her shuttle on the floor. Once when it fell she exclaimed she would marry the one who gave it back to her. At that very moment her dog Tumang, a demigod possessing magic powers, came up to her with the shuttle in his mouth. Dayang Sumbi had to marry him.
They lived happily together, and Dayang Sumbi gave birth to a baby boy, human in appearance but endowed with his father’s magic powers. She named him Sangkuriang. As the boy grew up, he was always guarded by the faithful dog Tumang, whom he knew only as a companion and not as his father, Sangkuriang became handsome and brave.One day his mother asked him to go hunting with the dog and bring her venison for a feast. After hunting all day withoutsuccess, Sangkuriang worried about facing his mother empty-handed. Desperate, he took an arrow and shot the dog. He returned home and handed over the meat to his pleased mother.
Soon after the feast, however, Dayang Sumbi questioned her son about the absence of Tumang. At first he evaded herqueries but finally told her what had happened. She was horrified and struck her son so hard on the temple that he
collapsed. For that, the old kinbanished his daughter from the court and she was made to roam around the kingdom. Sangkuriang recovered with a large scar on his temple, and he too left the court to wander about the world.
Years later, Sangkuriang met a beautiful woman and instantly fell in love with her. It was his own mother-they did not recognize each other. He pro¬posed to her and she agreed to marry him. On the day before the wedding, as she was caressing her fiancee’s hair, Dayang Sumbi detected the scar on the temple. Horror struck her, for she was about to marry her own son, Sang¬kuriang. Without revealing the whole truth to him, she tried unsuccessfully to dissuade him.When Dayang Sumbi saw that he was about to accomplish what she had thought impossi¬ble, she called on the gods to bring the sun up early and thwart Sangkuriang.
The cock crowed, the sun rose much earlier than usual, and Sangkuriang realized he had been deceived. In a fit of furyhe cursed Dayang Sumbi and kicked the half-finished boat back into the forest. There it lies upside down today, forming the mountain Tangkuban Perahu (Upturned Boat). Not far away is the stump of the tree Sangkuriang had felled, now called Bukit Tunggul. The dam Sangkuriang had built caused the valley to become a lake, where both Sangkuriang andDayang Sumbi drowned themselves. They were never heard of again.

Title: The Magical Forest Adventure

Once upon a time, in a small village nestled at the edge of a mystical forest, lived a curious young girl named Lily. She had always been fascinated by the tales of the enchanted forest that were passed down from generation to generation.

One day, while exploring the outskirts of the forest, Lily stumbled upon an ancient book hidden beneath a moss-covered stone. The book was bound in shimmering green leather and adorned with intricate gold designs. As she opened it, a bright light burst forth, and to her astonishment, the words on the pages seemed to dance and come alive.

Unaware of the magic within, Lily read the first page aloud, and suddenly, the forest transformed around her. Trees came to life, flowers bloomed in vibrant colors, and the air was filled with the sweetest scent she had ever experienced.

Startled yet thrilled, Lily decided to venture further into the forest, guided by the enchanted book. Along the way, she encountered friendly creatures like talking rabbits, wise owls, and mischievous fairies. They all welcomed her with warmth and led her deeper into the heart of the forest.

As Lily continued her journey, she discovered a hidden waterfall, cascading with sparkling water. The water was said to possess magical healing properties, capable of curing any ailment. Overwhelmed with joy, she dipped her hands into the glistening pool, and the magic of the water seeped through her fingers, filling her heart with an overwhelming sense of peace and happiness.

With each step, the forest revealed its secrets to Lily. She learned about the balance of nature, the importance of kindness, and the value of friendship. The enchanted creatures taught her ancient wisdom and shared tales of long-lost kingdoms that once thrived within the forest's depths.

Time seemed to fly as she spent days and nights in the magical forest, but soon, the enchanted book revealed that her journey was coming to an end. Reluctantly, she bid farewell to her newfound friends and thanked them for the unforgettable experiences they had shared.

As Lily returned to the village, she brought with her the wisdom of the magical forest. She shared the tales of her adventures, inspiring others to embrace the wonders of the world around them. From that day forth, the mystical forest remained a place of enchantment, but only those with the purest hearts and open minds could truly discover its magic.

And so, the legend of Lily's journey through the magical forest spread far and wide, becoming a timeless tale that captivated the hearts of all who heard it. To this day, the enchanting forest continues to whisper its secrets, waiting for the next adventurous soul to unlock its hidden wonders.

28. apa perbedaan antara narative text dan recount text


narrative : Memiliki tiga struktur kebahasaan yang terdiri dari Orientiation, Complication dan Resolution.
recount : Memiliki tiga struktur kebahasaan, yaitu Orientation, Event dan Reorientation.

Narrative Text Recount Text 01
Memiliki tiga struktur kebahasaan yang terdiri dari Orientiation, Complication dan Resolution.
Orientation merupakan tahap pengenalan tokoh oleh pencerita. Complication merupakan tahap dimana terdapat konfilik yang dialami oleh tokoh.Resolution merupakan tahap akhir dimana tokoh tersebut memecahkan konflik atau masalah yang diahapinya
Memiliki tiga struktur kebahasaan, yaitu Orientation, Event dan Reorientation.

Orientation merupakan tahap pengenalan tokoh oleh pencerita.Event merupakan rentetan kegiatan yang dilakukan oleh tokoh yang disajikan secara urut. Reorientation merupakan rangkuman atau kesan tokoh mengenai kejadian, kegiatan atau peristiwa yang diceritakan. 02 Narrative Text mengahadirkan konflik yang dialami oleh pelaku. Narrative Text menceritakan konflik tersebut kemudian menceritakan bagaimana untuk memecahkannya. Recount Text tidak menghadirkan konflik seperti di Narrative Text. Recount Text hanya menghadirkan rentetan kegiatan yang dilakukan oleh tokoh dalam waktu yang urut. 03

Deskripsi waktu pada Narrative Text kurang detail.Dalam Narrative Text tidak mencantumkan kapan kejadian yang diceritakan terjadi. Narrative Text hanya menuliskan "Once upon time" dan "Once day", untuk mendeskripsikan waktu.


Deskripsi waktu pada Recount Text lebih jelas dari pada Narrative Text. Jika pada Narrative Text hanya menggunakan "Once upon time", pada Recount Text biasanya menyebutkan hari seperti "on monday", "in the next day", "then", etc.

Once a rabbit was roaming near a lake in a forest. Suddenly,he saw a turtle and mocked him saying "Hurry up,you slow poke! Don't you find life very dull taking so long to cover a few yards? I could have run to the other side of the lake by now."

The rabbit agreed laughingly. In a few minutes he was away and out of sight.

"What a funny race" he said to himself , "I'm already half-way through. But it is very cold;why shouldn't I have a nap under the tree?"

The turtle walked steadily on and on. In a short time,he passed by the sleeping rabbit.

The rabbit slept far longer than he had intended. When he woke up at last,he looked around in surprise and said to himself,"Not even sign of the poor turtle anywhere I had better trot along and finish the race."

The rabbit ran to the finish line. He was amazed to see all the animals cheering the turtle who had arrived just a minute earlier. How ashamed he felt indeed.
